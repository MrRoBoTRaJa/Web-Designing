Report Summary

The report states that the trust was founded by social leaders, educationalists, and village or community leaders for sustainable development in Jharkhand, including work among vulnerable women, children, and underserved communities.

It presents a broad development agenda across education, welfare, technical training, social justice, environmental awareness, rural development, and health awareness.

Core Objectives

What the report says the trust works on.

The original report includes many objectives. These are grouped below into readable themes.

Educational institutions and student support

Establishing schools, supporting higher and technical education, scholarships, and educational opportunities for poor and needy students.

Employment and technical networking

Obtaining technical employment and training support through national and state institutions, and promoting vocational provider support.

Health, disability, and welfare

Awareness, identification, diagnosis, training, rehabilitation support, health camps, and aids for disabled persons.

Awareness, events, and public engagement

Sports, fairs, group discussions, debates, seminars, awareness campaigns, camps, and educational community activities.

Environmental and agricultural development

The report includes plantation, pollution control, irrigation, agriculture support, medicinal plants, horticulture, and farmer training.

Social protection and equality

It also mentions women security, equality, brotherhood, anti-trafficking, anti-dowry, anti-corruption, and support for tribal and backward communities.
